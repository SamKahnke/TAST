Our Story

Meet The Team

Kaylena Riendeau

Kaylena received a BA in English Education from Bethel University in Saint Paul, MN, and currently works towards a Master of Social Work at USC. Kaylena is passionate about educating teens on injustice within the school system. She founded TAST to empower students to be the hands and feet of Jesus by educating others within their schools, families, and communities. Kaylena has been involved in combating human trafficking for the past 7 years, and is devoting her life to this calling. She enjoys spending time with her family and friends, listening to Country music, traveling, and watching Gilmore Girls.

"If I can save one student from falling victim to trafficking, then I has been successful."

Correena Spangler

Correena graduated from the University of Sioux Falls with a degree in political and social science. As the curriculum writer, she believes in the necessity of discipleship paired with social justice. Correena has always had a heart for social justice awareness and education. She has been involved in anti-trafficking work via awareness campaigns and volunteering for the past three years. She enjoys hiking, baking, writing, antiquing and watching Sandra Bullock movies.

"If we try to do social justice without Jesus we will be doing it wrong, and if we do discipleship without social justice we will be ineffective."
